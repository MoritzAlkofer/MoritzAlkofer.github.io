  • Stigler's law

  • Wet bias
    Like everyone else, weather forecasters optimize for what they measure. One would assume that they measure forecasting accuracy. In fact, they measure viewer quotes. A viewer expecting a sunny day who is suprised by rain will be very annoyed, and switch forecaster. A viewer expecting rain, being suprised by a sunny day (delightful) is much less likely to change forecaster. Therefore, forecasters developed a 'wet bias', overpredicting the likelyhood of rain.
