Company size

at different company sizes you get confronted with different levels of structure.
at a small company, you have no structure at all.
at a mid sized company, you have some structure.
at a large sized company, you have a lot of structure.
at the largest companies, government, you have the most structure.
you can replace the word structure with bureaucracy.

The more structure you have, the more your success is measured in rules set by the structure, not the real world.
As such, the more structure increase, the more you can advance by complying with the rules, not the real world.

Of course, complying with the rules can mean to produce meaningful work. It can also mean gaming the system, via politics or producing visibility.

Language and symbols

What is language? A system of symbols that allow to communicate concepts. A good language allows to communicate concepts efficiently. To do so, a language uses symbols. Symbols represent concepts themself and are the essential unit of all communication. As such language is never made in a vacuum and depends on previously defined symbols. An interesting example of people thinking about symbols is the field of Nuclear semiotics. It tries to find symbols working without context to deter people from entering nuclear waste sites.

Bureaucrats

Personally, I am not a fan of the bureaucrat. Sometimes I try to fight them. For example I found great joy in reading the study regulations of my studies to look for loopholes. But at the end of the day I hope There would be less bureaucrats.
Most of the time each person is immersed in the details of one special part of the whole and does not think of how what they are doing relates to the larger picture. It is characteristic of most people to keep a myopic view of their work and seldom, if ever, connect it with the larger aims they will admit, when pressed hard, are the true goals of the system. this myopic view is the chief characteristic of the bureaucrat.
Richard Hamming, The art of doing science and engineering
