Lectures

  1. MIT lecture on how to speak by Patrick Winston
    Amazing lecture on how to give a good talk. It seems tremendously important not only to do cool things, but to communicate them to others in a way that makes them recognize their coolness, too.
    In his book `the art of doing science and engineering` Richard Hamming says: `All [methods of conveying ideas] are essential — you must learn to sell your ideas, not by propaganda, but by force of clear presentation. I am sorry to have to point this out; many scientists and others think good ideas will win out automatically and need not be carefully presented. They are wrong; many a good idea has had to be rediscovered because it was not well presented the first time, years before! \ New ideas are automatically resisted by the establishment, and to some extent justly. The organization cannot be in a continual state of ferment and change, but it should respond to significant changes.

Commencement Speeches

I know I know. Commencement speeches are cheesy. Old white man talking to young white man about his bright, prosperous future. But I think about it this way: People like Steve Jobs would sit down and think really hard about what they want to tell young people who just graduated. Turns out I just graduated. So you get advice from Steve Jobs , and alike specifically targeted to your situation. how cool is that?
